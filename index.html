About Us

Learn a little bit more about us!

NYU-Poly's Faculty Engineers in Residence (EIR) are faculty members who bring their entrepreneurial experience to teaching and research. The Faculty EIR will work to:

Advise students on how to promote the values of i2e, and act as mentors to young participants at the incubators.
Assist resident companies at the incubators where there is affinity with the fellow's academic work or entrepeneurial experience.
Offer periodic lectures at the incubators abour their own entrepeneurial experiences.
Invite colleagues and visitors to the inclubators for lectures and discussion.
Give feedback on activities of the student entrepeneurship club (EIA).
Serve as mentors to student teams involved in NYU's student entrepeneur competitions and help find appropriate faculty mentors for the various Poly teams.

Wayne Jones is a senior Computer Science student from NYU Poly. His love for web development and database management went beyond that of the college classroom. The Engineers in Residence (EIR) website was inspired by an idea that Steve Kuyan came up with while working at the Varick Street Incubator.
